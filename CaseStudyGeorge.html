Case Study

Technology can be used to spread awareness. International scientific communities should pay more attention on the residual heat or waste heat and investigate further how it impacts the local and global climate patterns through data analytics and manipulation.

The issue and implication of this, however, is the sheer difficulty of investment and enforcing everyone to switch completely.

The ReUseHeat team has worked on existing case studies where urban waste heat recovery is used as one of the project's initial outcomes instead of sending the heat back into the environment. According to the investigation, the temperature difference between the incoming wastewater temperature and the cooling water temperature should be as big as feasible in order to achieve the greatest performance from the heat recovery system. In order to effectively spread awareness, policymakers should consider how to switch the efforts and resources from focusing on GHG reduction to waste heat reduction efficiently by developing effective policies.
